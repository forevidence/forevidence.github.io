Your members. Your standard. Your IP.

Professional societies are the right bodies to define what competent AI practice looks like in their field. Building and running the credential program underneath that standard is a different job — and it shouldn't cost you ownership of your own intellectual property.

The situation

The authority is yours. The infrastructure is the problem.

Your members are asking what competent AI practice looks like in your field, and no one is better placed to answer than you. But turning that answer into a credential means assessment design, item banks, practical exam environments, proctoring, badging, verification, and an annual refresh cycle — a standing operation most societies don't want to staff and can't justify building from scratch.

The usual alternatives are worse. Platform vendors want your content on their terms. Co-branding deals quietly subordinate your name. And commercial training providers end up owning the curriculum your members' dues paid to develop.

Our position is simple.

You are the authority. We are the operator. Authority isn't transferable — and we don't ask you to transfer it. If you already publish competency recommendations, we extend them rather than replace them; an individual credential sits beside your program accreditation, not over it. Everything we build for you vests in you.

Nonprofit structure

Designed around your exempt status, not against it

A credential program touches your tax status, your liability exposure, and your governance in ways a normal vendor engagement doesn't. We design for those constraints from the first conversation, and we expect your counsel in the room.

  • Unrelated business income. Education and credentialing revenue structured to sit squarely within your exempt educational purpose; enterprise and bulk-licensing channels flagged early for tax review rather than discovered later.
  • Implied warranty exposure. Badge and credential language framed as competency assessed, not fitness for practice — the distinction that keeps a credential from reading as a guarantee about a member's work.
  • Conflict-of-interest firewall. If your society also authors standards that others accredit against, we help separate competency authorship from education revenue, and document the separation.
  • Sponsor influence. Sponsor and vendor capital admitted only downstream of methodology — labs, scholarships, convenings — never curriculum or exam design.
  • No private inurement. Our fees are for services rendered at arm's length. We hold no equity, revenue share of your mission income, or ongoing claim on program surplus.

We're not your tax or legal advisers — these are design constraints we build around, and your counsel signs off on the structure.

The pathway

A stackable route, shaped to your field

We don't arrive with a fixed ladder. How many steps there are, what each is called, and where the assessment bar sits are yours to set — they depend on your members' entry points, your CE/CME obligations, and what your accreditors already recognize.

What we bring is the design discipline behind stackable credentialing: making each step independently meaningful, ensuring the steps genuinely accumulate rather than merely sit alongside one another, and matching the rigor of the assessment to the strength of what each step claims about a person.

In practice that usually means shorter, focused steps for members who need one specific competence, and a culminating designation for those taking on formal oversight responsibility — with assessment becoming progressively more demanding as the claim gets stronger.

⬡

Design principles we hold to

  • Members can enter at more than one point
  • Every step is independently verifiable on its own
  • Steps accumulate toward a higher designation
  • Assessment rigor scales with the strength of the claim
  • Credit and CE/CME recognition can attach at any step
  • The whole route maps to your domains and to accreditor frameworks
How an engagement runs

Four phases, and you can stop after any of them

  1. Blueprint

    We map your competency framework to recognized standards and design the assessment architecture. You end this phase owning a blueprint you could hand to anyone.

  2. Build

    Curriculum, item banks, and the practical-exam environments — developed as work for hire, vesting in you.

  3. Launch

    Pilot cohort, psychometric review, standard setting, and issuance under your brand.

  4. Operate & refresh

    Delivery, proctoring, verification, and the annual content refresh that keeps AI material from going stale.
